## Assessment

Day 3 was operationally better than Day 2. The bot did not overbuild the book, the live monitor actively reduced unmanaged risk, and max-position gates prevented fresh buys once the portfolio was full. The open book was small and slightly green at the snapshot.

Trading performance was less impressive than the controls. `TSLA` and the first `HOOD` entry were cut for losses, while `MU` produced only a small locked-in gain. The current open book helped, but the bot-only session was still about breakeven to slightly negative at the snapshot.

The main remaining engineering issue is graceful operator shutdown. The Day 3 log appears to stop mid-cycle while the market was open, which makes reporting and risk management less clean than a controlled stop. A stop-request workflow has now been added so the running bot can receive a manual stop instruction, optionally flatten/cancel through Alpaca paper, remove the request file, and exit normally.

## Changes Applied From Day 3 Lessons

- Added a watched stop-request file for the autonomous trader.
- Added `scripts/request_day_trader_stop.py`.
- Added `--stop-file` to both day-trader launchers.

- Documented clean stop usage in `docs/autonomous_day_trader.md`.
- Added tests for stop-without-flatten and stop-with-flatten behavior.
